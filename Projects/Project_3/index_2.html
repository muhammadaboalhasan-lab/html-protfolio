Ingredients:

  • 1 package of biscuits

For strawberry syrup:

  • 170 g strawberries, washed and stemmed
  • 80 ml (1/3 cup) water
  • 65 g (1/3 cup) white sugar
  • White sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 package of biscuits
  • 2 tablespoons orange liqueur or brandy

For the cream cheese:

  • 330 g (1/3 cup) mascarpone cheese
  • 330 ml (1/3 cup + 1 tablespoon) heavy cream
  • 70 g (1/2 cup + 1 tablespoon) powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

For decoration:

  • 250 grams washed strawberries, sliced

How to prepare:

  1. Prepare strawberry syrup: Place the strawberries in the container of a food processor or blender and grind to a smooth puree.
  2. In a small saucepan, boil water, sugar and vanilla extract. Add the strawberry puree and orange liqueur or brandy and stir. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Pour the syrup into a wide bowl.
  3. Prepare mascarpone cream: Place all ingredients in the mixer bowl (or a bowl with a hand mixer) and beat until a firm cream is obtained.
  4. Line the pan with cling film and assemble the cake: Pour 1/3 of the cream into the pan and smooth out. Dip the biscuits in the strawberry syrup and place on the cream so that they cover the whole thing densely (cut them if necessary).
  5. Pour another 1/3 of the cream over the biscuits, arrange the biscuits dipped in syrup again and continue like this one more time (it's perfectly fine if the biscuits of the last layer protrude above the pan).
  6. Cover the cake with cling film and store in the freezer for 4 hours to overnight.
  7. Invert the pan onto a suitable serving plate, remove the cake and remove the plastic. Place in the refrigerator until the cake is completely thawed.
  8. Garnish the cream with fresh strawberries, slice and serve.
